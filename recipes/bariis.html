Bariis

Description

The second, Bariis Iskukaris (cooking rice and sauce together) is the most common and it's cooked with tomatoes, onions, garlic, meatballs, and a mixture of spices called xawaash (a mix of ground cumin, turmeric, coriander, paprika, cardamon, black pepper, cloves, cinnamon, and nutmeg).


Ingredients

  • Tomatoes
  • Rice
  • Paprika

Steps


  1. Rinse 2 cups of basmati or long-grain rice until water runs clear. Soak for 20–30 minutes.
  2. Chop:
    1. 1 onion – finely chopped
    2. 2–3 garlic cloves – minced
    3. 2 tomatoes – chopped or blended
  3. Prepare xawaash spice mix:
    1. 1 tsp cumin
    2. 1 tsp turmeric
    3. 1 tsp coriander
    4. 1 tsp paprika
    5. ½ tsp cardamom
    6. ½ tsp black pepper
    7. ¼ tsp ground cloves
    8. ½ tsp cinnamon
    9. Pinch of nutmeg
  4. Heat 2–3 tbsp of oil in a pot.
  5. Sauté chopped onions until golden.
  6. Add minced garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  7. Add tomatoes and cook until they break down into a saucy texture (5–10 minutes).
  8. Add xawaash spices to the sauce and toast for 1–2 minutes.
  9. (Optional) Add meatballs or meat chunks and brown them in the sauce.
  10. Pour in 3½ to 4 cups of water or stock. Add salt to taste.
  11. Bring everything to a boil.
  12. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the pot. Stir well.
  13. Let it boil uncovered until the water level reaches the surface of the rice (about 5–7 minutes).
  14. Reduce heat to low, cover the pot tightly, and steam for 15–20 minutes until rice is fully cooked and water absorbed.
  15. Fluff the rice gently with a fork or spoon.
  16. (Optional) Garnish with:
    1. Fried raisins
    2. Fried onions
    3. Boiled eggs
    4. Fresh salad
  17. Serve hot.
  18. Reflect:
    1. Did you nail the rice-to-water ratio?
    2. Did you sauté onions properly before adding garlic?
    3. Were spices toasted or burnt?
    4. Did you resist the urge to lift the lid while steaming?
    5. Would a stoic warrior cry over soggy rice? Only if he let himself go soft.
